C-suite leaders awake.

"Our SI presents great slides every two weeks, but I cannot tell if we're actually ready."

SI presentations are designed to protect the engagement. An independent readiness assessment gives you the view your SI cannot give you.

"We're 18 months into D365 and nobody can tell me who owns the chart of accounts."

This is the most common single point of failure in ERP programs. Ownership gaps are not a people problem. They are a process problem with a systematic solution.

"We hired ex-Big4 people internally. The program is still struggling."

Because the problem is not methodology. It is that nobody is asking the uncomfortable questions about what is actually owned, what is actually clean, and what will break at go-live.

"We're spending $500K/month on the SI and I can feel something is wrong but I can't name it."

That feeling is usually right. The gap is almost always the same: process ownership, master data readiness, and controls: the three things that live outside the SI's statement of work.

"My auditors keep finding SOD violations after go-live. How do we stop that cycle?"

By mapping segregation of duties before go-live, not discovering violations in the audit. The methodology exists. The discipline is what's missing.

"We want to deploy AI on top of our new ERP, but our data is still a mess."

AI on dirty data is just faster wrong answers. Master data readiness is the prerequisite nobody builds into the AI project plan. Until now.

AI Enablement

Most companies are spending on AI.
Very few are getting ROI from it.

The promise of AI in the enterprise is real. The results most organisations are seeing are not. Not because the technology fails. Because the foundation it needs to run on was never built. Dirty data produces confident wrong answers. Undocumented processes get automated in their broken state. AI agents make fast decisions with no human governance in place to catch what they get wrong.

True AI ROI is not a technology problem. It is a readiness problem. And it requires the same discipline that every successful enterprise transformation requires: clean data, documented processes, defined ownership, and governance that tells the AI where to act and where to stop and ask a human.

Thriven helps C-suite leaders build the foundation that makes AI investments deliver.

I

AI Readiness Assessment

Which processes in your organisation are genuinely ready for AI automation, and which will fail because the data or the governance is not there yet. We give you a clear map before you spend the budget.

II

Human-in-Loop Design

For every automated process, there is a point where a human must stay in the loop. We identify where those points are, design the governance around them, and ensure your AI deployment does not create uncontrolled risk.

III

Data Foundation for AI

AI is only as good as the data it runs on. We assess your master data readiness, identify what needs to be cleaned and governed before AI deployment, and stop you from automating chaos at speed.

IV

AI Governance Framework

Who owns each AI decision. What gets audited. What triggers human review. What constitutes a control failure. The governance framework that makes your AI investment defensible to a board, an auditor, or a regulator.

The organisations getting real ROI from AI are not the ones with the biggest AI budgets. They are the ones that did the unglamorous work first: clean data, owned processes, governed decisions. That work is what Thriven does.
